 //abstract

 

 "For me as for so many other artists, limitations really help to drive the creative process. In my case one could even say that hindrances and rules like time, cost and size are necessary to get anything done at all. And the more difficult and hard these restrictions are, the more satisfying and fun I find it is to work within and against them. I will talk about self-imposed rules and show examples of works were constraints have been crucial. The examples range from tiny little 140 character long code compositions, up to my grand 40-year plan guiding my artistic activities and daily life."

 //limitations

 

 but unlimited possibilites scares me!

 i get nothing done. to be creative i need...

 

 laws

 rules

 borders

 barriers

 hindrances

 constraints

 limitations <- (more and more i think this is the one for me)

 restrictions

 malfunctions

 ...







 

 in 'normal' computer programs the limitations

 are predefined. (which is often a good thing)

 as noted i get creative when there are limits.

 

 in 'creative coding' programs there is little

 limitation and great freedom. (which is not

 always a good thing) as noted i get confused

 when there are no rules, no limits.
















 so learning to code...

 

 != be instantly free and creative

 

 == be able to lock yourself into a customly

    suited creative situation


 == learn about your own creativity



 double hard...

 A - learn coding really well

 B - create your own limits










 so, the main reason i have struggled and learned

 to use all the open systems above is that they

 let me construct unique rules and limitations.
